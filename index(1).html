Unit 1: Introducing Ourselves

Learn how to boldly introduce yourself to new classmates!

1. Greetings & Your Name

To start an introduction, always use a Greeting first. Here are some excellent options:

"Hello everyone!" (Friendly)
"Good morning! Let me introduce myself."

To express your identity, formulate your sentences using these structures:

• My name is [Your Name] • I am [Your Name]

Example: "My name is Bagas." or "I am Bagas."

2. Origin, Age & Hobbies

Tell your friends where you come from and what you enjoy doing in your free time!

Origin & Living Place:
"I live in Yogyakarta." or "I am from Depok."
Age representation:
"I am 13 years old."
Hobbies & Activities:
"My hobby is reading." or "I like cycling."

Grammar Focus: Subject & Possessive Pronouns

To talk about other people, we must choose the correct pronoun based on their gender. Analysis of pronouns is key to polite speech!

Target Person Subject Pronoun Possessive Pronoun Introduction Example
A Boy / Man (Male) He His "This is Arka. He lives in Depok. His hobby is gaming."
A Girl / Woman (Female) She Her "This is Zahra. She is 13. Her favorite book is Harry Potter."
More than one person (Plural) They Their "These are my friends. They are twins. Their hobby is swimming."

Social Context Analysis: Formal vs Informal

Analytical thinking requires knowing **when** to use a specific expression. Introducing yourself to a **teacher** is different from introducing yourself to a **classmate**!

Formal Context (e.g., Teachers, Headmaster)

Use elegant and high-respect expressions:

  • "Good morning, Sir/Ma'am."
  • "Allow me to introduce myself, my name is..."
  • "It is an honor to meet you."

Informal Context (e.g., Peers, Friends)

Use warm, casual, and energetic greetings:

  • "Hi guys!", "What's up, everyone?"
  • "I'm Bagas. You can call me Bagas."
  • "Nice to meet you all!"
